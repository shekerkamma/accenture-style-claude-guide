# Engagement Kickoff

## When To Use

Use in the week before an engagement begins. The kick-off sets the relationship tone, the operating model, and the shared understanding of what success looks like. Engagements that skip a structured kick-off spend their first four weeks resolving ambiguity that a good kick-off would have eliminated in four hours.

## Consulting Approach

A kick-off has three jobs: align on the objective and scope (what are we here to do?), establish how we will work together (governance, cadence, escalation), and build the relationship (client and consulting team trust begins here). The mistake is treating it as a project management exercise — the kick-off is the first client impression, and it sets the expectation for everything that follows.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the scope, objectives, and success criteria with the engagement lead before the kick-off date.
2. Design the kick-off agenda: balance strategic alignment with practical working model setup.
3. Prepare the team: brief every team member on their role, the client context, and the win themes from the proposal.
4. Facilitate the kick-off: align on objectives → establish governance → agree ways of working → define the 30-day plan.
5. Capture decisions and open items in a kick-off record distributed within 24 hours.
6. Confirm the first three steering meetings, working group cadences, and key milestone dates.

## Quality Bar

- Success criteria must be agreed with the client at the kick-off, not defined by the consulting team alone.
- Governance cadence must be confirmed with calendar invites before the kick-off ends — a cadence that isn't booked isn't a cadence.
- The 30-day plan must contain deliverables, not activities — what will exist at the end of each week.
- Kick-off record must be distributed to all attendees within 24 hours while the agreements are fresh.
