# Progress Reporting

## When To Use

Use throughout the engagement delivery phase on a weekly (working group) or monthly (steering committee) cadence. Progress reports that are honest about Amber and Red status are the most effective client relationship tool available — they build trust precisely because they surface problems early, before they become crises.

## Consulting Approach

A progress report has one job: give the reader what they need to make a decision or take an action. The mistake is writing a report that makes the team look busy rather than one that makes the client's decision-making easier. RAG status must be honest — a report that is perpetually Green is either a very easy engagement or one where problems are being hidden. Both are dangerous.

## Workflow

1. Collect status from each workstream lead: milestone progress, RAID updates, decisions needed, resource issues.
2. Assign RAG status for each workstream and for the overall programme.
3. Identify the top 3 issues or risks that require client or leadership attention.
4. List decisions needed from the client or steering committee, with a decision deadline.
5. Compile the horizon look: what is coming in the next two weeks that the client should be aware of.
6. Distribute within 24 hours of the close of the reporting period.

## Quality Bar

- RAG status must be assigned by evidence, not optimism — if a milestone is at risk, it is Amber regardless of whether the team believes it will recover.
- Decisions Needed section must specify a "needed by" date — decisions without deadlines do not get made.
- Budget actuals must be current as of the reporting date, not the previous month's close.
- Report must be distributed within 24 hours of period close — a status report delivered three days late is not a status report.
