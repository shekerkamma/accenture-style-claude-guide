# Stakeholder Cadence

## When To Use

Use at engagement kick-off to design the meeting architecture, and revisit at every stage gate as the engagement moves between phases. A cadence that made sense in the diagnosis phase may be wrong for the delivery phase. The most common engagement management failure is a steering committee that receives status updates but never makes decisions — this skill is designed to prevent that.

## Consulting Approach

Stakeholder cadence has two distinct purposes: governance (decisions, escalation, accountability) and collaboration (working, co-creation, problem-solving). These require different forums, different attendees, and different agenda designs. The steering committee is a decision forum, not a status update meeting. Working groups are collaboration forums, not reporting sessions. Conflating the two produces meetings where nothing gets decided and nothing gets done.

## Workflow

1. Map all stakeholders by influence, engagement level, and information need.
2. Design governance forums (steering, sponsor 1:1) for decision-making and escalation.
3. Design collaboration forums (working groups, workstream syncs) for delivery and co-creation.
4. Define agenda templates for each forum — the agenda is the contract of what the meeting is for.
5. Design the information flow: what feeds up from working groups to steering, what decisions flow down.
6. Confirm attendance, frequency, and duration for each forum.

## Quality Bar

- Steering committee agenda must contain explicit decision items — a steering agenda with no decisions is a status meeting in disguise.
- Sponsor 1:1 must be owned by the consulting engagement lead, not delegated to the project manager.
- Escalation path must define resolution SLAs — an escalation path with no timelines is not a governance mechanism.
- Every forum must have a named facilitator and a named note-taker — these are different people.
