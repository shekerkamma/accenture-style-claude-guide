# Engagement Closeout

## When To Use

Use in the final two weeks of any engagement. Closeout is the most commonly skipped engagement management activity — teams focus on delivering the final output and declare victory without capturing what was learned, securing the client relationship, or positioning the next opportunity. A well-executed closeout is worth months of business development.

## Consulting Approach

Engagement closeout has four objectives: deliver the final output cleanly (no loose ends), capture institutional knowledge before the team disperses, secure the client relationship for future work, and harvest the reference case and lessons that make the next similar engagement better. The sponsor 1:1 at closeout is the most important conversation of the engagement — it determines whether this client becomes a reference and whether there is a follow-on.

## Workflow

1. Confirm all contractual deliverables are complete and formally accepted by the client.
2. Conduct a client satisfaction conversation with the engagement sponsor — structured, honest, and forward-looking.
3. Capture team lessons: what worked, what didn't, what would be done differently.
4. Document the engagement as a reference case: client name (or anonymised), challenge, approach, outcome, team.
5. Identify follow-on opportunities: what did this engagement reveal that the client still needs?
6. Archive all engagement materials in the firm's knowledge management system.

## Quality Bar

- Client satisfaction conversation must be conducted in person or by video — a survey form is not a relationship conversation.
- Reference case must be approved by the client before being used externally — verbal approval noted in the record.
- Follow-on opportunities must have a named owner and a next action — an opportunity list with no owner is a wish list.
- Engagement economics must be final-closed before the team is released — cost surprises discovered after closeout cannot be recovered.
