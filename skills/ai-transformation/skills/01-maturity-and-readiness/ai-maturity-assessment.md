# AI Maturity Assessment

## When To Use

Use at the start of an AI strategy engagement before recommending investments or use cases. An organisation that believes it is at Level 3 (Scaling) but has Level 1 data infrastructure will fail to realise AI value regardless of the use cases chosen. The assessment anchors the strategy in what is actually possible, not what is aspirational.

## Consulting Approach

Accenture's AI maturity model assesses five dimensions independently — because an organisation can be advanced in one (e.g. a strong data team) while lagging in another (e.g. no AI governance). Each dimension has four levels: Exploring (ad hoc), Building (piloting), Scaling (systematic deployment), and Leading (continuous reinvention). The overall maturity is the lowest-scoring dimension, not the average — a chain breaks at its weakest link.

## Workflow

1. Identify the scope: whole enterprise, a specific business unit, or a function.
2. Score each of the five dimensions on a 1–4 scale using evidence from interviews, documentation, and observed practice.
3. For each dimension, identify the specific blocker preventing progression to the next level.
4. Calculate the overall maturity level (minimum dimension score) and the composite score (average).
5. Map the organisation's position against industry peers where benchmarks are available.
6. Identify the two or three investments that would have the highest leverage on overall maturity progression.

## Quality Bar

- Scores must be supported by evidence — interviews, system access, documentation — not self-assessment surveys alone.
- Overall maturity is the minimum dimension score, never the average; document this clearly.
- Blockers must be specific: "no data governance policy" not "data is a challenge."
- Recommendations must address the actual blockers identified, not generic AI best practices.
