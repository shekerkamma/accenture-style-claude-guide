# Capability Maturity Progression

## When To Use

Use annually as the strategic review of a deployed capability, or when a capability appears to have stalled — users are using it but outcomes are not improving. Maturity progression is what separates organisations that continuously improve a capability from those that deploy it and walk away.

## Consulting Approach

Capability maturity models define progression as a sequence of qualitatively different operating modes, not just "doing more of the same better." The transition from Level 2 to Level 3 is not incremental improvement — it requires a different way of working, different governance, and often different technology. The most common stall point is Level 2 (Repeatable) because organisations optimise what they have instead of redesigning for the next level.

## Workflow

1. Select or define the maturity model appropriate to the capability (use the 5-level CMM structure: Initial → Repeatable → Defined → Managed → Optimising, or an AI/domain-specific model).
2. Assess current maturity level across each dimension of the capability using evidence, not self-assessment.
3. Identify the target maturity level for the next 12–18 months (usually one level up, sometimes two for high-priority capabilities).
4. Identify the specific blockers preventing progression to the next level.
5. Design the maturity progression roadmap: what investments, process changes, and governance changes are required.
6. Define the leading indicators that will signal the organisation is on track to reach the next level.

## Quality Bar

- Current maturity must be assessed by evidence, not leadership aspiration — score what is observed, not what is intended.
- Blocker analysis must reach the root cause — "lack of investment" is a consequence, not a blocker.
- Progression roadmap must be sequenced by blocker resolution, not calendar convenience.
- Leading indicators must be observable before the next annual review — lagging indicators confirm the result; leading indicators allow course correction.
