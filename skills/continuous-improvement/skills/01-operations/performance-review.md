# Performance Review

## When To Use

Use quarterly as part of the operating rhythm, or when performance is materially below target and a structured diagnosis is needed. The quarterly business review is the primary instrument for catching benefit drift before it becomes permanent — organisations that review performance annually cannot course-correct in time to recover missed targets.

## Consulting Approach

A performance review is not a status update — it is a diagnosis. The structure moves from what (the metrics), to why (the root cause), to so what (the implication), to now what (the action). A review that stops at "what" produces a report. A review that reaches "now what" produces a plan. The test of a good performance review is whether it changes what the organisation does next quarter.

## Workflow

1. Collect performance data for the period: KPI actuals vs. targets, benefits actuals vs. register, operational metrics.
2. Identify material variances: any metric more than 10% off target warrants a root cause analysis.
3. For each material variance, complete a structured root cause analysis.
4. Assess the trend: is performance improving, stable, or deteriorating?
5. Prioritise corrective actions by impact and urgency.
6. Update the benefits register with revised forecasts based on current trajectory.

```markdown

## Executive Summary

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

- **Root cause:** [Specific cause — not "challenging conditions"]
```

## Quality Bar

- Variance threshold must be applied consistently — do not selectively analyse only convenient variances.
- Root causes must be specific and actionable — "market conditions" and "user adoption challenges" are not root causes.
- Trend assessment must distinguish structural underperformance from transient variance — the corrective action is different for each.
- Benefits forecast must be updated every quarter — a benefits register that has not been revised since go-live is a fiction.
